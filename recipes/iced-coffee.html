Iced Coffee Recipe

Description:

Iced coffee is a chilled, invigorating beverage made by brewing coffee and then cooling it over ice. It offers a refreshing alternative to hot coffee, with a smooth, bold flavor that's often enhanced by a splash of milk or cream and a touch of sweetener. Perfect for a warm day, iced coffee delivers a crisp, energizing kick while keeping you cool.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup (240 ml) brewed coffee (hot or cold)
  • 1/2 cup (120 ml) milk or cream (adjust to taste)
  • 1-2 tablespoons sugar or sweetener (to taste)
  • Ice cubes
  • Optional: flavored syrups or extracts (e.g., vanilla, caramel)

Steps:

  1. Brew 1 cup of your favorite coffee. You can use a coffee maker, French press, or any preferred method. If youâ€™re using hot coffee, allow it to cool slightly before proceeding.
  2. While the coffee is still warm, stir in 1-2 tablespoons of sugar or sweetener, depending on your taste. This helps the sweetener dissolve better.
  3. Let the coffee cool to room temperature, or place it in the refrigerator to speed up the cooling process.
  4. Fill a glass with ice cubes.
  5. Pour the cooled coffee over the ice. Add milk or cream to taste, stirring gently to combine. If you prefer a flavored iced coffee, you can also add a splash of flavored syrup or a few drops of extract at this stage.
  6. Stir the coffee well and serve immediately. Enjoy your refreshing iced coffee!

Feel free to adjust the milk and sweetener amounts according to your preference. You can also experiment with different types of milk or non-dairy alternatives for a personalized touch!
